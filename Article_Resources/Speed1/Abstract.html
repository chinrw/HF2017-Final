The exhaust system of a typical automobile includes a catalytic converter followed by a muffler. Internal combustion engines, commonly used by automobiles, are a source of both air and noise pollution. The catalytic converter and muffler address these problems. Each component is essential but requires considerable space in the vehicle, and both can create backpressure which reduces the engine’s efficiency. Miguel Santos’ “Silencer and Power Enhancement Environmental Device (SPEED)” patent is a device which functions both as a catalytic converter and a muffler. The initial design of this device was unsuccessful, therefore, improvements on his design and further research into the two devices were necessary. Alumina and stainless steel ATI 409HP are materials that, according to research, should better suit this device. The geometry was also modified, considering structural integrity while maintaining a succession of chambers with increasing cross sectional area. Inserting wash coated ceramic filters into the channels of this updated SPEED device promises adequate catalysis and conversion of exhaust materials. In the future, a prototype built with these conditions can be used to obtain quantitative data on its performance. Although this device may seem more convenient, it might not work as efficiently as current exhaust devices working in succession.
